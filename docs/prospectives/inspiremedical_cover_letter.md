To the Inspire Medical Systems hiring team,

Inspire's founding principle, "If we put the patient first, we will never lose our way," is the kind of mission that gives infrastructure work meaning beyond uptime metrics. Elevating and redefining the standard of care for obstructive sleep apnea demands reliable, secure, and well-architected platforms behind the scenes, and that is exactly the kind of engineering I have spent my career delivering. I am writing to express my interest in the Platform Architect role.

For the past six years at Optum, the technology arm of UnitedHealth Group, I have been one of the deepest technical owners of an enterprise internal Kafka platform: 1,000+ production nodes, 750+ clusters across GCP, five nines reliability, zero customer data loss. The platform is built on custom Kubernetes operators in Go, a CRD-driven provisioning framework, Terraformed cloud infrastructure, and a self-service developer portal. I design reusable Terraform modules across GKE, VPC, DNS, IAM, and Cloud Storage; build Kubernetes controllers managing stateful workloads; review and guide technical designs across the team; and produce the documentation, runbooks, and architecture records that make the platform maintainable.

The aspect of this role that resonates with me most is building reusable frameworks and golden paths. I am consistently the first responder when company wide mandates land before any standard exists. I rebuilt our container supply chain for Chainguard hardened image adoption before a formal golden path existed. I triaged and resolved hundreds of cloud infrastructure security findings in days when an internal detection system hard launched with no warning. I led Grafana consolidation from self hosted pods to a centralized enterprise offering. In each case the pattern is the same: absorb an ambiguous directive, decompose it into scoped engineering work, build the repeatable path, and bring the broader organization along. That is exactly what a Platform Architect does: translating theory into production implementations that other teams can adopt.

Security is foundational to what I build. This platform carries PHI/PII healthcare data under strict compliance requirements. I own the distribution and rotation of client mTLS certificates, enforce encryption at rest via GCP KMS and in transit via TLS, manage Vault secrets and IAM policies, and coordinate with data governance teams on legally approved access workflows. The regulated data handling discipline I have built in healthcare translates directly to the compliance and quality standards of a medical device company like Inspire.

I care deeply about mentorship and making platforms understandable to the teams that depend on them. I captain a team of 6 engineers, write user stories, conduct code reviews, and author technical documentation used across the organization. I volunteer for Optum Early Careers each year, running bootcamp lectures and conducting candidate interviews. Inspire's commitment to honesty, integrity, and patient centered quality signals a culture where engineering rigor and clear communication are valued, and that is where I do my best work.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Caleb Kopp
