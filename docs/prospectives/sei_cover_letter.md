I am writing to express my interest in the Senior Software Engineer role at SEI. As WorkforceEdge scales and moves away from custom-per-client implementations toward a sustainable, unified service model, I bring direct experience doing exactly that: decomposing tightly coupled systems into extensible microservice architectures while keeping the solution as simple as possible. Complexity is easy; simplicity under pressure is the real engineering challenge, and that is the challenge I gravitate toward.

At Optum, the technology arm of UnitedHealth Group, I serve as lead engineer and product owner of a Kafka-as-a-Service platform spanning 1,000+ production nodes, 750+ clusters, and five-nines reliability with zero customer data loss. I write production code daily in TypeScript, React, NextJS, and Node.js for the platform's self-service micro-frontend, and in Go and Terraform for the backend microservice layer. The platform's architecture is built on independently deployable services connected through typed API contracts that decouple product experience from infrastructure. This is the same contract-driven decomposition that turns monoliths into maintainable, extensible systems: define clean service boundaries, express interactions as versioned contracts, and let each team evolve their service independently without cross-cutting regressions. I have lived that journey at enterprise scale, and I would bring those patterns directly to WorkforceEdge.

The experience most relevant to SEI is my track record leading cloud migration and platform simplification. I am a Google Cloud Certified Digital Leader, with formal training and certification in digital transformation strategy, cloud adoption frameworks, and infrastructure modernization, including monolith-to-microservice decomposition and GCP hosting models (GKE, Anthos, Compute Engine, App Engine, Cloud Run). As an SE at Optum, I led our platform's first move from on-prem to the cloud. We started with a pilot on Azure, generating customer Terraform to provision VM ScaleSets. I discovered undocumented API rate limits in Azure through hands-on network traffic analysis and request header inspection - limits that were not in their documentation, not surfaced in any dashboard, and not disclosed in our official engagements with their engineering team. I combined that finding with a cost comparison showing GCP offered significantly lower pricing for equivalent compute specs and presented the case to platform leadership. That investigation steered the team to adopt GCP as our primary cloud host, a decision that cascaded across all of Optum's data platforms and drove millions of dollars in cumulative infrastructure savings. The following year was spent migrating customers off on-prem clusters, shared-tenant deployments, and bespoke self-hosted instances onto the automated self-service GCP product I built the portal for. SEI's own move to GCP maps directly to this experience. I know what it takes to evaluate cloud providers with honest data, build the case for change, execute the migration without disrupting customers, and own the developer experience that drives adoption on the other side.

On the application side, I built a full-stack SaaS application using React, TypeScript, Express/Node.js, TypeORM, and MSSQL that replaced a manual spreadsheet process with a standardized data model and RESTful API. In my current role, I design microservice boundaries and API contracts, manage SQL-backed data stores through ORMs, and automate delivery through GitHub Actions CI/CD pipelines, all core to the WorkforceEdge stack. I believe strongly in test-driven development and keeping solutions as straightforward as the problem allows; the best code is the code you do not have to write.

I captain a team of 6 engineers using Agile methodology, writing user stories, conducting code reviews, and shipping features iteratively. I lead through technical depth, not title, and I welcome being pushed on my thinking; the best solutions come from honest technical discourse, not ego. I mentor junior engineers through Optum's Early Careers program each year and coordinate across product, security, and compliance stakeholders daily. I would bring that same collaborative, simplicity-first mindset to SEI from day one.
