To the SONIFI Health hiring team,

That philosophy resonates deeply with how I approach platform engineering at Optum, the technology arm of UnitedHealth Group. The systems I build serve healthcare teams and the patients behind them; the best infrastructure is the kind that disappears behind a simple, reliable experience. That conviction, and SONIFI Health's commitment to being a trusted partner that delivers promises made, anticipates needs, and grows alongside its customers, is what draws me to the Solutions Architect role.

At Optum I architect and own a Kafka-as-a-Service platform: 1,000+ production nodes, 750+ high-throughput clusters across multi-tenant cloud environments, five nines reliability, zero customer data loss. The platform is built on custom Kubernetes operators in Go, typed API contracts that decouple the product experience from infrastructure internals, Terraformed cloud infrastructure, and a self-service developer portal I lead as product owner. I make architecture decisions daily that balance reliability, scalability, security, and operability, and I communicate those tradeoffs to engineering, product, security, and compliance stakeholders at every level. This is not theoretical architecture; I write and ship production code across every layer of the stack.

The architecture challenge that maps most directly to SONIFI Health's needs is the full-stack Warpstream delivery I led. In 8 weeks I architected and delivered a net-new product across every layer: a Go operator from scratch, all Terraform cloud infrastructure (Cloud Storage, VPC, DNS, IAM), integration into our self-service portal, and full observability with Prometheus, Grafana, and PagerDuty. This was not migration work; it was designing and building a new solution from the ground up to solve a concrete business problem, reducing annual infrastructure costs by approximately 80%. That ability to take a product requirement, decompose it into an architecture, and personally deliver the solution end to end is exactly what I understand SONIFI Health needs in its next architect.

My platform also handles 3rd party integrations and secure cross-system communications that parallel SONIFI Health's integration challenges. I architect and maintain integration points with external vendor APIs, certificate-governed schema management services, and a secure mTLS certificate authority that generates and rotates thousands of client certificates. Every data stream carries PHI/PII requiring end-to-end SSL/TLS transport, encryption at rest, IAM controls, and legally-approved data governance workflows. I have spent six years designing secure communications between on-prem and cloud systems in a regulated healthcare environment, and that experience transfers directly to architecting secure, compliant integrations between SONIFI Health's on-prem systems, cloud infrastructure, and healthcare customer environments.

I captain a team of 6 engineers and lead through depth, not title: code and design reviews, mentoring on distributed systems and Go, and coordinating across engineering, product, security, and compliance. I consistently volunteer as the first responder when company-wide mandates land before any established path exists, decomposing ambiguous directives into scoped deliverables and running focused calls to unblock teams. SONIFI Health's belief that being a good partner means "delivering promises made, anticipating your needs and exceeding your expectations, evolving and growing with you" describes how I strive to work with every team I serve. I am excited by the opportunity to bring that approach to SONIFI Health and help build the architecture that powers better healthcare experiences for patients and clinicians alike.

Thank you for your time and consideration.
