To the Balyasny hiring team,

Balyasny is founded on principles of partnership and humility, with a culture built around breaking out of silos to bring ideas together. That philosophy resonates deeply with how I think about platform engineering: the best infrastructure disappears behind great automation so the teams it serves can focus on generating alpha, not filing tickets. I am writing to express my interest in the Senior Kafka Platform Engineer role.

For the past six years I have lived inside a Kafka platform at enterprise scale, and the thing that keeps me engaged is not keeping the technology ecosystem healthy but the puzzle of making a complex distributed system feel simple to the people who depend on it. I have done this from the ground up. When our platform was on premises only, I led the migration to cloud, built the cost case that saved the organization millions, and helped design the operator framework that turned manual provisioning into a portal click. Wherever Balyasny's streaming platform is today, whether it is early stage, partially automated, or already mature, I am confident I can meet it there and build it toward enterprise grade self service provisioning that is secure, observable, and cost effective.

What I find most compelling about this role is the breadth of ownership. My current work spans the full lifecycle: writing Go operators that manage stateful workloads, authoring all Terraform for cloud infrastructure, designing failover topologies with Beam and MirrorMaker 2, and rotating mTLS certificates across hundreds of clusters carrying regulated data. Security and cost discipline are not afterthoughts in my work; they are constraints I design around from day one. My platform carries PHI/PII healthcare data under strict compliance requirements, and the Warpstream product I coled from scratch is projected to cut annual Kafka infrastructure costs by 80%. I thrive when I can own a problem end to end rather than hand pieces off across team boundaries. That matches how Balyasny's Technology teams are described: building systems with efficiency and scale, directly enabling investment teams to make quick, accurate decisions.

The story I am proudest of is not the biggest number on my resume; it is one of the harshest time's I failed. When I was tasked with benchmarking Warpstream against Apache Kafka, my initial test methodology produced results that both Confluent and our leadership questioned. Rather than defend a flawed approach, in a quick turn around I redesigning the entire framework, added consumer workloads and latency measurements, and delivered findings that held up to scrutiny from both sides. That willingness to iterate, take feedback without ego, and produce rigorous results under pressure is something I would bring to Balyasny every day.

I lead a team of 6, mentor junior engineers, and run oncall across the full fleet, but I still write production code daily. I care about developing the people around me as much as developing the platform, and Balyasny's meritocratic culture and investment in talent development signal that those values would be shared. I greatly look forward to speaking more with the team about how I can contribute to Balyasny's streaming platform and the success of the investment teams it serves.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Caleb Kopp
