To the Confluent hiring team,

Confluent describes its platform as a central nervous system for companies, connecting applications around real time data streams so organizations can react intelligently to everything happening in their business. I have spent the last six years learning exactly how true that is. At Optum, the technology arm of UnitedHealth Group, Kafka is the backbone that every data producing and consuming team depends on. When streaming works, teams build confidently. When it does not, nothing else matters. That experience, seeing firsthand what it takes to make data in motion reliable at enterprise scale, is what draws me to the Senior Software Engineer II role.

Rather than purchasing a managed Kafka platform, my team built one from scratch on open source Apache Kafka: custom Kubernetes operators in Go, our own automation and observability, a self service developer portal, and a two tier distributed control plane delivering 1,000+ nodes and 750+ clusters across GCP with five nines reliability and zero customer data loss. We license Schema Registry and Warpstream from Confluent, but the deployment tooling, Helm charts, operators, and everything around them is ours. That means I have spent my career solving the same class of problems Confluent solves: how do you make streaming infrastructure invisible to the teams that depend on it? How do you give developers a provisioning experience that "just works" while enforcing security, compliance, and reliability underneath? Those are the questions I think about every day, and I would love to be solving them at the source.

Warpstream is where that ambition becomes concrete. I led the full stack delivery of Warpstream based cluster provisioning pipeline: a net new Go operator, all Terraform cloud infrastructure from scratch, and full observability integration. Before that, I solely owned Warpstream viability performance testing, coordinating directly with Confluent engineering and Optum leadership, iterating on methodology when initial results were questioned, and producing a report that directly informed the decision to offer Warpstream in production here at Optum. I have experience coordinating with your engineering team, have shipped Kafka to production customers, and am eager to deepen my knowledge of Confluent's products. The chance to work with the source of truth for streaming infrastructure, to learn from the best in the business, and to contribute to a product that I have seen solve real problems for real customers is an opportunity I am very excited about.

What I would bring to Confluent is not just operational depth but the perspective of someone who has built and operated enterprise streaming platforms and partnered with application teams to deliver value with real time data. I know what enterprise platforms need because I have been the one building them for our customers: typed API contracts that decouple product experience from operator internals, certificate governed schema management, mTLS across PHI/PII data streams, runbooks and incident response playbooks, and tooling that reduces operational toil between incidents. During our platform's migration from on prem to GCP, I discovered undocumented Azure API rate limits through hands on investigation and presented analysis that steered the team's cloud vendor decision, a shift that saved millions in infrastructure costs. That drive to dig in, find the real answer, and improve the system is something I would bring from day one.

I captain a team of 6 engineers and lead through technical depth, not title: code and design reviews, mentoring on distributed systems and Go, and coordinating across engineering, product, security, and compliance. I consistently volunteer as the first responder when company wide mandates land before any established golden path exists. I run technical bootcamp lectures and conduct candidate interviews for Optum Early Careers each year, because growing the people around you matters as much as shipping features. "No egos, no solo acts; just smart, curious people pushing toward something bigger, together." This is how I strive to work every day, and it is why Confluent is where I want to be.

Thank you for your time and consideration.
